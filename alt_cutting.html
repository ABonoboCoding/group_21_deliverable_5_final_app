Cutting

Chop

Chopping is probably the most used and "informal," if you will, of the techniques. It refers to cutting food into small, irregular pieces. Most recipes will specify what size pieces are required, with coarse (3/4") and fine (1/4") chop being common. If no number is given, go with 1/2" pieces.

Dice

Unlike a chop, a dice refers to cutting food into small uniform or dice-like pieces, ranging from 1/8" - 1/2". With dicing comes some waste, since you'll need to cut the vegetable or fruit into a block shape, but the extra pieces can be reused for stock or other dishes. One you have your block formed, slice the blocks into strips in the desired thickness and then cut again into smaller pieces to create cubes.
